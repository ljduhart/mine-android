Child Safety Policy

Last updated: April 27, 2026

Our Commitment

Mine is a dating platform designed exclusively for adults aged 18 and older. We maintain a zero-tolerance policy toward Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE) of any kind. The safety and protection of children is a core priority, and we are committed to preventing our platform from being used to harm, exploit, or endanger minors in any way.

Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Mine:

  • Any content that depicts, promotes, or glorifies the sexual exploitation or abuse of children, including Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM).
  • Solicitation, grooming, or any behavior intended to sexually exploit a minor.
  • Sharing, distributing, or requesting imagery or content involving the sexual abuse of children.
  • Any attempt to use Mine to contact, recruit, or communicate with minors for exploitative purposes.
  • Content that sexualizes minors in any form, including illustrations, AI-generated imagery, or fictional depictions.

Age Verification

Mine requires all users to be at least 18 years of age. We employ age verification measures during account registration and reserve the right to terminate any account found to belong to a minor or any account engaged in activity that endangers children.

Detection and Reporting

We actively monitor for CSAE-related content and activity. When we identify or receive reports of potential CSAM or child exploitation, we take the following actions:

  • Immediately remove the content and disable the offending account.
  • Report the incident to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) via their CyberTipline, as required by U.S. federal law.
  • Cooperate fully with law enforcement investigations.
  • Preserve relevant evidence as required by applicable law.

In-App Reporting

Users can report suspected CSAE content or behavior directly within the Mine app using the Report feature available on every user profile and in every chat conversation. All reports related to child safety are treated as high priority and are reviewed promptly.

Contact Us

If you become aware of any child safety concerns related to Mine, please contact us immediately:

  • Email: safety@mine.dating

For emergencies involving a child in immediate danger, please contact your local law enforcement or call 911.

Industry Standards

Mine's child safety practices are informed by the principles and frameworks published by the Technology Coalition and align with applicable child protection laws, including U.S. federal reporting obligations under 18 U.S.C. § 2258A.
